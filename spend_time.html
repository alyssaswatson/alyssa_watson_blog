Where Should I Spend My Time?

I think one of the concepts that I have been struggling the most with in the past couple of weeks, is not a coding concept at all. I have been thinking about where to spend my time when learning to code. I currently have a full time job, a couple of side projects, and I spend 20 hours a week in my Full Stack Web Development program. I also want to spend time with my family and take care of myself (imagine that, I like to eat). I have a lot to do, and not as many hours in the day as I would like. I’m not claiming to be an expert by any means, but I’ve put together a list of a few things I strive to do to keep myself on track:

  • Schedule everything - I live by my google calendar. If I have an appointment, I put it on my google calendar so I don’t forget about it. Why not do the same for work I need to get done? If I put it on my calendar for a specific time, I will make time to “show up” for that project.
  • Say “NO” - I have found that if I spend the majority of my time and energy on the tasks that I WANT to be doing, and not things I feel like I HAVE to do, I am a much happier person. This means saying “no” to tasks you don’t feel drawn to. Just because someone asked you to do it, does not mean you have to accept.
  • Know when to move on - When I am stuck, sometimes the best thing to do is move on and come back to it. I could spend hours and hours looking at three lines of code and never seeing what is wrong, but sometimes if you step away and work on something else, you can come back to it later with fresh perspective.
  • Make enough time to recharge - You will be so much more productive if you take care of yourself. If you get enough sleep, eat balanced meals, and make enough time to recharge you will not only feel better but you will be better at your work.

Hopefully, making this list will inspire me to be better with my time. These are things that I strive to do all the time, but just like everything else, some days are easier than others, so don’t forget to give yourself some slack, and listen to your body. If you are working too hard, take a break, you will be able to accomplish so much more if you listen to your body.
